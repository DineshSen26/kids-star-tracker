How to Use {{ app_name }}

A quick guide for parents and kids. Follow these steps to set up your family and start earning stars.

1

Create your account

Register with email and password, or sign in with Google. One parent account can manage up to 5 kids.

{% if not current_user.is_authenticated %} Register {% endif %}

2

Add your kids

Go to Kids, add each child's name and age, and pick a fun avatar. You can also turn on optional PIN login so a child can open their own task page safely.

3

Create tasks with icons

Go to Tasks, type a task name like "Brush Teeth", and the app auto-suggests icons. Kids see a large picture on their task card — perfect for children who do not read yet. Assign tasks to one or more kids.

4

Kids complete tasks

Open a child's Tasks page from the dashboard. They tap Complete when done. The app plays a cheerful sound, shows confetti, and awards stars for that day.

5

Set up rewards

Go to Rewards and create prizes like "Movie Night" or "Ice Cream" with a star cost. When a child has enough stars, you can redeem the reward from the rewards page.

6

Track progress

Use the Dashboard for charts, streaks, badges, and a leaderboard. Check History to see completed tasks by day, week, or month — and export a CSV if you want a record.

Features

Visual task icons

Tasks show big icons so young kids understand what to do without reading.

Star rewards

Each task earns stars. Balances are tracked in a clear ledger.

Redeemable rewards

Kids save stars and unlock treats or privileges you define.

Dashboard & charts

See daily, weekly, and monthly progress at a glance.

Streaks & badges

Stay motivated with streaks and fun achievement badges.

Child PIN login

Optional PIN keeps a child's task page private on shared devices.

Celebrations

Confetti, sounds, and encouraging voice messages when tasks are done.

History & undo

Review completed tasks and undo mistakes if needed.

Dark mode

Toggle light or dark theme from the navigation bar.

Tips for Parents

  • Start with 3–5 simple daily tasks so kids are not overwhelmed.
  • Use higher star values for harder tasks and lower values for quick habits.
  • Let kids help choose reward goals — they stay more excited that way.
  • Open the child task page on a tablet and let them tap Complete themselves.
  • Check the dashboard together at the end of the day to celebrate wins.
